7. How Table 6 Is Calculated (Deep Dive)

Table 6 shows the percentage of each team that is fully productive in every month across the selected quarter timeline. The model builds that value in stages, using the inputs in Sections 1–5.

  1. Translate product demand into required headcount. Each quarter rate from Section 2 is applied to every month in that quarter. For each team, the required headcount for that month is estimated by interpolating between the “Products / Quarter” staffing rows in Section 1 (linear between levels, and bounded at the minimum/maximum levels). If the required headcount is below the current staff, the current staff is used instead (no negative hires).
  2. Derive monthly hires. Starting from the current headcount, the model adds hires only when the required headcount is higher than the prior month’s total. The monthly hires are therefore:
    Monthly hires = max(required headcount − prior total headcount, 0)
  3. Split hires into high-fit and low-fit cohorts. The “Internal hiring (%)” inputs in Section 3 are now set per team and per quarter, and each value is treated as the share of hires who are high-fit. For each month:
    High-fit hires = hires × internal hiring share
    Low-fit hires = hires − high-fit hires
  4. Apply the training curve. Section 4 provides the productivity (%) for each cohort by month since hire. For a given calendar month, each prior hire cohort contributes:
    Effective capacity = high-fit hires × high-fit curve + low-fit hires × low-fit curve
    The “month since hire” index is the distance between the hire month and the current month.
  5. Compute the proficiency percentage. For each month, the model sums total headcount (current staff + all hires) and effective capacity (current staff + trained productivity of hires). The table value is:
    Proficiency % = effective capacity ÷ total headcount
    The conditional formatting thresholds in Section 6 (red/orange) are then applied to these percentages.
  6. Estimate trainee capacity. A trainer is someone already in the team before the timeline starts, or a hire cohort that has reached 100% proficiency. The actual headcount entered in Section 5 caps how many trainers can be present in the team. The percentage input in Section 6 defines how much trainer time one trainee requires in normal operations; trainee capacity is then estimated as trainers × (100 ÷ trainer-time-per-trainee%).
  7. Compute training-adjusted newness and compare it to capacity. Training-adjusted newness is the absolute number of people still not fully trained, calculated as Σ(cohort size × (1 − cohort proficiency by month-since-hire)). Team shrinkage removes the newest cohorts first, so people still in training leave before fully proficient cohorts.
    Total training-adjusted newness = high-fit training-adjusted newness + low-fit training-adjusted newness
    Absolute proficiency = total headcount − min(total headcount, training-adjusted newness)
    Capacity is treated as a hard comparison metric: cells are highlighted red when untrained team members > training capacity. For the effective team members table, conditional coloring is now applied only when effective team members are below required monthly staffing from Sections 1–2 (after quarter-to-month conversion), with the Section 6 red/orange thresholds determining severity.
